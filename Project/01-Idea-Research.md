# Niche Research — Service Business Ideation

**Profile:** Web Designer, 2+ yrs experience | Prilep, North Macedonia | 20h/week | $0 capital  
**Goal:** 3 niches with domain access, real buyers, real money. No trends, no AI gimmicks.

---

## Niche 1 — Local Trade Businesses (HVAC, Plumbers, Electricians, Roofers)

These businesses make $150k–$2M/year, have terrible websites, and are bleeding leads to HomeAdvisor. They pay $200–500/month for leads they should be getting for free. Your web skills are a direct solution to their most expensive problem.

| Job | WHO has this pain | WHAT they do now | WHY it matters NOW | $10–20 entry offer | WHERE to find 20 today |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Their business is invisible on Google Maps** | Owner-operators, 1–5 staff, 45–60 yrs old, zero tech knowledge, $150k–$800k revenue | Pay HomeAdvisor / Angi $200–500/mo for leads, or do nothing and rely on word-of-mouth referrals | Google Maps is now the #1 source of local service leads. Every month offline = customers going to the competitor two listings above them | **$20 Google Business Profile audit PDF** — lists exactly what's missing and broken. Upsell: $150 setup + $50/mo management | Google Maps: search "plumber [any US city]" → filter by <15 reviews or missing photos → find contact on their site → cold email |
| **Website is broken or invisible on mobile** | Same owner, site built by a nephew in 2018, never touched since | Nothing. They don't know slow = lost calls. Some complain their "website doesn't bring business" | Google's Core Web Vitals penalizes slow mobile sites. 65% of local searches are on phone. They're ranking below competitors and don't know why | **$15 PageSpeed + mobile audit report** with 3 specific, jargon-free fixes listed. Upsell: $100–200 one-time fix | Run any local business from Yelp or Yellow Pages through PageSpeed Insights — most will score 20–40/100. That's your cold-outreach list |
| **No way for customers to book after hours** | Any trade service that takes appointments (HVAC tune-ups, electrical inspections, cleaning) | Rely on voicemail. Miss 30–50% of after-hours leads. Some pay an answering service $100/mo | Home service apps (Thumbtack, TaskRabbit) are eating their lunch specifically because they offer instant booking. Every missed call is $150–500 in lost work | **$20 Calendly embed setup** on their existing site — 1–2 hours work, sell for $100–150, no new tech required | Google "[service] [city]" → visit each site → if there's no "Book Now" button, they're a prospect. Also check Nextdoor Business section |

## Niche 2 — Real Estate Agents (US / UK / AU markets)

Individual agents earn $40k–$200k/year in commission. They spend heavily on lead gen and personal branding. Every listing is a marketing event — and most agents handle it with a Zillow link and a PDF. You can change that with basic web skills.

| Job | WHO has this pain | WHAT they do now | WHY it matters NOW | $10–20 entry offer | WHERE to find 20 today |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No branded property page per listing — sending buyers to Zillow** | Solo agents and teams of 1–3, US suburbs, earning $60k–$180k GCI per year | Send a Zillow link or a Canva PDF to buyers. No lead capture, no personal brand on the listing | Agents who send buyers to Zillow lose the lead to Zillow. Every listing is a chance to capture buyer emails — most agents waste it completely | **$20 listing page template** (Carrd or a simple HTML file they self-fill). Or charge $40–75 per listing to set it up. Recurring: 1 agent = 10–30 listings/year | Realtor.com → click any active listing → check if agent has a branded property URL. If it's just a Zillow link, they're a prospect. DM on Instagram or LinkedIn |
| **Website looks like it was built in 2014** | Agents who've been in the business 5–10 years. Good reputation, strong reviews, embarrassing website | Avoid sending prospects to the site. Apologize for it verbally. Know they need a new one, keep procrastinating | 96% of buyers Google the agent before calling. A 2014 website signals "this person is out of touch." It costs deals silently — they never know why the phone stopped ringing | **$15 homepage redesign mockup in Figma** — "here's what your site could look like" as a pitch tool. Closes the conversation. Real project: $400–900 | Google "[agent name] realtor" for agents with 50+ Zillow reviews but ugly sites. Those agents have proof of income and a clear problem. Find email on their site or LinkedIn |
| **Not showing up on Google for "[city] real estate agent"** | Established agents who've never touched SEO. Paying $300–800/mo to a generic SEO agency that doesn't understand real estate | Overpay a generalist SEO agency that sends monthly PDF reports with no results | NAR commission settlement (2024) forced agents to differentiate on personal brand, not just MLS access. Organic Google visibility is now a direct revenue driver | **$20 local SEO audit** — which keywords they could rank for, what 3 pages are missing, what's broken in their Google Business listing. Upsell: $150–300/mo ongoing | Search "[city] real estate agent" — agents not on page 1 are your prospects. Find them on Zillow page 2–5. Cold email from their site or DM on LinkedIn |

## Niche 3 — Freelance Web Designers & Small Agencies (White-Label Subcontracting)

These are people exactly like you, 2–5 years ahead. They're drowning in client work and turning down money. You have their exact skillset, you speak their language, and they need help today. Geographic arbitrage works in your favor: US designers charge $75–150/hr and can resell your work at a margin.

| Job | WHO has this pain | WHAT they do now | WHY it matters NOW | $10–20 entry offer | WHERE to find 20 today |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **More client requests than capacity — turning down projects** | Solo freelance designers, 2–6 years experience, US/UK/CA, earning $50k–$120k/year | Refer to friends randomly, say no, or take on too much and burn out. Lose $2k–$8k in revenue per project they decline | Web design demand keeps growing. AI tools make clients request more builds, not fewer. Solopreneurs want websites faster than a solo designer can deliver alone | **$20/hour white-label rate** to be their silent subcontractor. They keep the client relationship and margin; you do the build. Entry offer: "I'll do one page free so you can see my quality" | r/webdesign, r/freelance (search "looking for subcontractor" or "overflow work"); Designer Hangout Slack #jobs channel; post in Facebook group "Web Design Business Owners" |
| **Ongoing client retainers eating all their time with small changes** | Designers who sold $200–400/mo maintenance retainers to 5–15 clients | Do it themselves, resentfully. Spend 3–5 hours/week on content swaps, plugin updates, text edits for $20–50 in effective hourly rate | AI tools mean clients now ask for more changes more frequently. A designer with 10 retainer clients spends 40+ hours/month on $20 tasks instead of $150/hour project work | **$15–20/hour overflow rate** for maintenance tasks. Offer a trial: take 3 clients for one month, prove reliability. They will not go back | Upwork: search "agency looking for developer subcontractor"; Contra platform freelancer directory; Twitter/X search "web designer overwhelmed" or "need help with client work" |
| **No polished proposal template for a new vertical they're targeting** | Designers expanding from generalist work into a niche (real estate, restaurants, health, law) | Write proposals from scratch every time. Spend 2–4 hours. Often skip them entirely and quote verbally, then lose the deal to someone with a slick PDF | Productized services are exploding. Clients comparing 3 proposals will pick the one that looks professional. A blank Google Doc loses to a branded proposal every time | **$15 industry-specific proposal template** (Notion or Google Docs) — fills 80% of the work automatically. Sell on Gumroad. 500 designers × $15 = $7,500 with zero recurring work | r/freelance, Gumroad search "web design proposal template" (check comments for buyers), Indie Hackers #freelancing tag, Lemon Squeezy marketplace |
