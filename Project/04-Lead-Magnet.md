## 2. CONTENT

### How to use this template

This template has 6 sections. Each has instructions in plain text and a completed example underneath. Read the instruction once, delete it, replace it with your own details. The whole process takes under 10 minutes.

Two fully completed example proposals are at the end of this document — one for a website redesign, one for a brand identity project. Use them as a reference while you fill in your own.

### THE TEMPLATE

---

**[YOUR NAME OR STUDIO NAME]**
**Proposal for [CLIENT NAME]**
*Prepared [DATE] · [PROJECT TYPE]*

---

#### Section 1 — Project Overview

**Instructions:** 2–3 sentences. Start with the client's goal, not your process. Show you understood their situation. Never open with "I" or "We will."

> *Write 1 sentence about their current problem. Write 1 sentence about what the project will achieve. Write 1 sentence about the specific outcome they care about — more leads, more sales, stronger brand.*

**Example:**
> Your current website was built in 2019 and no longer reflects the quality of work your studio produces. This redesign will create a modern, conversion-focused presence that positions you above local competitors and turns visitors into inquiries. The goal is a website that works as hard as you do — building trust on the first visit and driving qualified leads every month.

---

#### Section 2 — Scope of Work

**Instructions:** Bullet list of specific deliverables. Be concrete — not "homepage design" but exactly what that includes. Each bullet should be something you can check off as done. Group into 2–3 categories if the project has multiple workstreams.

> *List every deliverable. If it's not here, it's not included.*

**Example:**

**Website Design & Development**
- Custom homepage — hero, services, social proof, CTA sections
- 4 inner pages — About, Services, Portfolio, Contact
- Mobile-responsive across all screen sizes
- Contact form with email notification
- Speed and performance optimization

**SEO Foundation**
- Meta titles and descriptions for all pages
- Heading structure and alt text
- Google Search Console setup and initial indexing

---

#### Section 3 — Investment

**Instructions:** Pick a specific number — not a range. A range signals uncertainty. Price in the upper half of your comfort zone. Use line items so the client sees what they are paying for, not just a total. Label it "Investment", not "Cost."

> *List 3–5 line items. Add the total at the bottom.*

**Example:**

| Item | Investment |
|---|---|
| Website design (6 pages) | $1,400 |
| Development & responsive build | $600 |
| SEO foundation setup | $200 |
| 2 rounds of revisions | Included |
| **Total Investment** | **$2,200** |

*50% deposit to begin. 50% on project completion.*

---

#### Section 4 — Timeline

**Instructions:** Break the project into 3–4 named phases. Give each a specific duration, not vague language like "a few days." Include a start date and estimated completion date. If you don't have a start date yet, write "Start date: TBD upon deposit."

**Example:**

| Phase | What happens | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery & Wireframes | Research, sitemap, wireframe layout | Week 1 |
| Design | Homepage and inner page designs, client review | Week 2 |
| Development | Build, responsive implementation, forms | Week 3 |
| QA & Launch | Testing, fixes, go-live | Week 4 |

**Estimated start:** Upon deposit receipt
**Estimated completion:** 4 weeks from start

---

#### Section 5 — What's Included / What's Not Included

**Instructions:** The "What's Included" list reminds the client what they're getting. The "What's Not Included" list protects you from scope creep — the number one cause of underpaid projects. State these matter-of-factly, not defensively.

**What's Included**
- All items listed in the Scope of Work above
- 2 rounds of revisions per page
- 30 days of post-launch support for bug fixes
- Final files and assets delivered on completion

**What's Not Included**
- Copywriting or content creation (client provides all text and images)
- Logo design or brand identity work
- Ongoing maintenance after the 30-day support window
- Third-party tool costs (hosting, domain, plugins)

---

#### Section 6 — Next Steps

**Instructions:** Give the client exactly 3 steps to move forward. Be specific. End with one confident closing sentence — not a question, not "I look forward to hearing from you."

**Example:**

1. Reply to confirm you'd like to move forward
2. Invoice for the 50% deposit will be sent within 24 hours
3. Kick-off call scheduled for [DATE] to align on goals and timeline

Projects at this scope typically book 1–2 weeks out — securing your start date now ensures we hit your target launch.

---

**NOVA Studio**
**Proposal for Jordan Blake Fitness**
*Prepared 21 May 2026 · Brand Identity*

---

**Project Overview**

Jordan Blake Fitness has outgrown its current DIY logo and inconsistent visual identity — clients are booking despite the brand, not because of it. - Typography system (heading font + body font + rules)
- Brand voice guidelines (3 adjectives, tone examples)

*Deliverables*
- Full brand guidelines PDF (12–16 pages)
- All logo files in PNG, SVG, and PDF formats (light and dark versions)
- Social media profile picture and cover photo templates (Canva-editable)

|---|---|

| Brand guidelines document | $300 |

| Business card design | $100 |

*50% deposit to begin. 50% on final file delivery.*

**What's Included**
- Everything listed in Scope of Work above

Strong brand identity before a launch or relaunch consistently increases conversion on coaching offers — the sooner the brand is locked in, the sooner it starts working.
